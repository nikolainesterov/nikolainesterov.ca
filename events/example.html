Piano Recital · Master of Music

Master's Recital

Nikolai Nesterov presents his Master of Music graduation recital at the University of Calgary, featuring a programme of works that have defined the arc of his graduate studies. The evening spans three centuries of the piano repertoire, from the formal grandeur of the Classical era to the searing emotional landscape of the late Romantic.

The programme opens with a late Beethoven sonata — a work of stark contrasts and searching introspection — before moving into the crystalline world of Debussy's Préludes. The recital concludes with a major Romantic work, chosen to showcase the full range of the instrument and of Nikolai's artistic voice.

This recital is presented in partial fulfillment of the requirements for the Master of Music degree in piano performance under the supervision of Henry Kramer.

Programme
L. van Beethoven
Piano Sonata No. 31 in A-flat major, Op. 110
I. Moderato cantabile molto espressivo · II. Allegro molto · III. Adagio ma non troppo — Fuga
C. Debussy
Préludes, Book I (selection)
La cathédrale engloutie · La fille aux cheveux de lin · Ce qu'a vu le vent d'ouest
S. Rachmaninov
Piano Sonata No. 2 in B-flat minor, Op. 36
I. Allegro agitato · II. Non allegro — Lento · III. Allegro molto
